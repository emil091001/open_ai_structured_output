Data quality:
Specific data for the product composition are provided by the manufacturer. They represent the production of the declared product and were collected for
EPD development in the year of study. Background data is based on registered EPDs according to EN 15804, Ostfold Research databases, ecoinvent and other
LCA databases. The data quality of the raw materials in A1 is presented in the table below.
Specific environmental data from EPDs (Skanska EPD), in accordance with EN 15804, have been used for aggregates. Similarly, specific data have been used
for transport distances from supplier to asphalt plant and for energy use at factory. Other data (e.g. waste) are estimated. Specific data from the supplier have
also been used for bio-based binder. For all other data, generic data available in EPD tool v4.0 have been used. Transport of reclaimed asphelt from the road
to asphalt factory includes a return distance.
For bitumen, generic data from Eurobitume is used because specific data cannot be determined from the mix of bitumen suppliers according to EU
standards etc. used by Skanska.

Additional technical information:
Bio-based binder contains biogenic carbon equlvalvent to 20,2 kg CO2-e per tonne asphalt. At the end-of-life stage, the asphalt material is milled and
recycled into new asphalt mixes. Hence, the biogenic carbon serve as a long-term carbon sink. If considering the long term biogenic sink as negative to
GHG-emissions from A1-A3, the net sum is -3,3 kg CO2-e per tonne asphalt.
